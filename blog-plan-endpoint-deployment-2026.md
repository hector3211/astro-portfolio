# Blog Plan: Modern Endpoint Deployment in 2026

## Focus
Personal career narrative about rebuilding an MSP's workstation/endpoint deployment process in under a year. The story centers on **individual accomplishments**, decisions, and lessons learned - not the company as a whole.

## Core Achievement
Revamped workstation setup at an MSP, including:
- Endpoint/workstation deployments
- Windows and macOS staging policies
- Automations for repeatable setup tasks
- Cross-platform consistency

---

## Title Options

1. **"How I Rebuilt an MSP's Endpoint Deployment Process in Under a Year"**
2. **"I Modernized My MSP's Windows and macOS Staging in Less Than a Year - Here's What I Learned"**
3. **"What 2026 Endpoint Deployment Looks Like: Lessons From Rebuilding a Workstation Setup Solo"**
4. **"One Year, One Engineer: Rebuilding Endpoint Deployment From the Ground Up"**

Recommended: Option 1 or 2 for clarity and personal ownership.

Avoid language that frames the prior process as broken or chaotic. The MSP was operating successfully; this post is about how I evolved the workflow to match where the business was heading.

- MSP technicians and engineers
- IT managers running endpoint deployments

## Tone and Angle

## First-Person Language Guide

Use first-person active ownership. The company is the setting, not the hero.

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| "We decided to move to policy-driven deployment" | "I pushed for policy-driven deployment because..." |
| "Our team rolled this out" | "I built and rolled this out, then trained the team on it" |
| "The company saved time" | "The process I built cut deployment time by..." |
| "We standardized our image" | "I created the standard configuration policy that..." |

### Language to Avoid (Protects the MSP's Reputation)

| Avoid | Why | Try Instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| "chaotic" | Disparages the prior process and the team that built it | "manual," "technician-driven," "built up over time" |
| "broken" | Implies the MSP was failing | "not set up to scale," "hard to keep consistent" |
| "hope nothing got missed" | Suggests carelessness | "varied from machine to machine" |
| "fix" | Centers the old process as wrong | "modernize," "evolve," "improve," "standardize" |
| "still mostly manual" | Sounds like the MSP was behind | "relied on a lot of manual steps" |

## Sample Opening

> When I joined my current MSP, workstation deployments were handled technician-by-technician, with a lot of manual steps built up over time. It worked, but it didn't scale well - and it was hard to keep every machine consistent. Within my first few weeks, I made it my project to modernize the workflow. Less than a year later, I'd rebuilt our endpoint staging process for both Windows and macOS into something repeatable and easy to hand off.

---

## Key Takeaway to Reinforce

> In 2026, workstation deployment is less about building the perfect image and more about building repeatable, policy-driven workflows that work for both Windows and macOS.
